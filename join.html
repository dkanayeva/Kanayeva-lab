Openings

Join Us

We're an interdisciplinary group of three PhD candidates, an MSc student, and a rotating cohort of undergraduate researchers, working at the intersection of electrochemistry, molecular biology, and diagnostics engineering. We look for curiosity, patience with finicky lab electronics, and a genuine interest in sensors that reach patients outside major hospitals.

Current openings

Open

PhD position — EIS aptasensor development

Focus on aptamer selection and sensor optimization for infectious disease targets. Background in biochemistry, electrochemistry, or biomedical engineering preferred.

Rolling

Undergraduate & MSc research projects

Short- and long-term projects on sensor fabrication, data analysis, and assay validation. Open to NU Biology and MSc in Life Sciences students.

How to apply

  • Email a CV and a short note on why our work interests you to the address on the Contact page.
  • PhD applicants should also mention prior lab experience (electrochemistry, molecular biology, or biosensor work is a plus, not a requirement).
  • We reply to every serious inquiry, though it may take a couple of weeks during teaching periods.

What you'll learn

Hands-on aptamer design and selection, electrochemical impedance spectroscopy and potentiostat operation, sensor surface chemistry, and how to take a lab result toward something that could actually work in a clinic. Group members are also mentored on scientific writing, conference presentation, and grant writing.
