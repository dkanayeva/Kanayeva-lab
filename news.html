Lab updates

News

Milestones, awards, talks, and other updates from the group.

Mar 2026

Awarded the Academician Nailya Bazanova Medal

Dr. Kanayeva received this national recognition from the National Academy of Sciences of the Republic of Kazakhstan for outstanding scientific achievements and contributions to the advancement of science.

2026

New review published in TrAC Trends in Analytical Chemistry

"Advancements and Strategies in Simultaneous Multi-Target Detection for Analyte Multiplexing," with Zhazira Zhumabekova and Nigara Yunussova, published in a top-1% journal (IF 12).

Sep 2025

Keynote lecture in Vietnam

Dr. Kanayeva delivered a keynote, "Aptamer-Based Rapid Detection of Biomarkers in Infectious Diseases and Cancer," and chaired a plenary session at the 3rd International Symposium of Nano Life Science in Qui Nhon, Vietnam.

Fall 2025

New students join the lab

Zhazira Zhumabekova began her PhD and Kamshat Kozybayeva began her MSc in Life Sciences, joining ongoing PhD candidates Kamila Sagdat and Meruyet Issimova.

Aug 2025

Recognized for 14 years of service at Nazarbayev University

Awarded a medal marking 14 years of dedication and service, alongside a Certificate of Appreciation for NU's 15th anniversary.

Summer 2025

🎓 Congratulations to Dr. Nigara Yunussova

Nigara defended her PhD thesis, "Characterization and Application of ssDNA Aptamers for the Detection of Carcinoembryonic Antigen," capping a research journey that began as an MSc student in 2018 and included first-author work on the group's CEA aptasensor and multi-target detection review.

Jul 2025

🏆 PhD students win awards in Beijing

At the A5+N International Summer School (China Agricultural University), Zhazira Zhumabekova took 1st place for her oral presentation and 3rd place for her poster; Kamila Sagdat also placed 3rd for her poster — both fully funded to attend through the lab's international collaborations.

2025

Dual-pathogen aptasensor paper published

"Simultaneous and sensitive detection of Mycobacterium tuberculosis and SARS-CoV-2 antigens employing an electrochemical impedance spectroscopy aptasensor," published in Frontiers in Bioengineering and Biotechnology.

Apr 2025

Hosted Prof. Man Bock Gu (Korea University)

Dr. Kanayeva organized a visit and lecture, "Aptamer-based Biosensors for Point-of-Care Testing Applications," by the distinguished biosensor researcher from Korea University.

Fall 2024

Two new PhD students join the lab

Kamila Sagdat and Meruyet Issimova began their doctoral work in the group.
