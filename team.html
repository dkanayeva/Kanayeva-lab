The people

Group Members

A small, close-knit group spanning undergraduate researchers to postdocs — with a long track record of training women in electrochemical biosensing.

Principal Investigator

DK

Dr. Damira Kanayeva

Associate Professor · Department of Biology, Nazarbayev University

Ph.D. in Cell and Molecular Biology, University of Arkansas (2010). An accomplished academic leader with 15 years at Nazarbayev University's Department of Biology as full-time faculty — teaching microbiology and molecular biology there continuously since 2011, where this lab has always been based — and a record of institutional service spanning the University's founding years to the present. Alongside her faculty role, she also founded a biosensors laboratory at what is now the National Laboratory Astana — known at the time as the Center for Energy Research, later renamed NURIS before becoming NLA — directing it until 2015, including two years also serving as Acting Director of NU's central Interdisciplinary Instrumental Center during its founding years (53 research staff across eight laboratories, two State Targeted Research Programs, and shared research-grade instrumentation enabling cross-departmental graduate training).

Research focuses on aptamer-based electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) sensors for cancer biomarkers and infectious disease diagnostics — tuberculosis, SARS-CoV-2, and monkeypox virus. Holds a granted Kazakhstani patent for tuberculosis-detecting ssDNA aptamer sequences (No. 33639) and has graduated four PhD students to date, with three additional PhD candidates currently ongoing, backed by approximately USD 1.6M in competitive research funding as PI/Co-PI.

Recognized with the Academician Nailya Bazanova Medal (2026) and a Distinguished Researcher in Natural Sciences award (2021). Currently provides university-level academic leadership as Chair of the Biology Department's Curriculum Committee and as a member of the Faculty Senate and Academic Council, building on more than a decade of continuous institutional and national service in curriculum governance, faculty recruitment, accreditation (ABET/ANSAC), and research administration.

Beyond the lab, has served as Faculty Advisor to NU's Kazakhstan team at the iGEM Giant Jamboree (2013; Silver Medal, 2015) and mentors NU SciPod, a student-run science podcast, since 2024.
