About the lab

Sensors that turn a binding event into a decision

We design aptamer-based electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) biosensors — label-free, low-cost, and fast enough for point-of-care use. An aptamer, a short strand of DNA or RNA selected to bind one target with high specificity, is anchored to an electrode surface. When the target binds, it changes the electrode's impedance signature, which we read directly with a potentiostat. No fluorescent tags, no lengthy sample prep, no centralized lab required.

Dr. Kanayeva holds a Ph.D. in Cell and Molecular Biology from the University of Arkansas and joined Nazarbayev University's Department of Biology as full-time faculty in 2011, teaching microbiology and molecular biology there continuously since — this lab has always been based in that department. Alongside her faculty role, she also founded a biosensors laboratory at what is now the National Laboratory Astana — known at the time as the Center for Energy Research, later renamed NURIS before becoming NLA — directing it until 2015, including two years also serving as Acting Director of NU's central Interdisciplinary Instrumental Center during its founding years (53 research staff across eight laboratories). The group's ssDNA aptamer sequences for Mycobacterium tuberculosis secreted protein MPT64 are the basis of a granted Kazakhstani patent, and the multichannel aptasensor built on them for simultaneous detection of M. tuberculosis and SARS-CoV-2 antigens was published in Frontiers in Bioengineering and Biotechnology in 2025.

Research directions

Where our sensors are headed

Cancer diagnostics

ssDNA aptasensors for carcinoembryonic antigen (CEA), from aptamer selection through electrochemical detection, published in iScience (2024).

Infectious disease

Multichannel EIS aptasensors for tuberculosis, SARS-CoV-2, and monkeypox virus — including simultaneous multi-pathogen detection on a single chip.

Multiplexing & detection strategy

Methods for simultaneous multi-target analyte detection, surface chemistry optimization, and aptamer selection to push specificity and shelf life.

Why it matters

Built for places centralized labs don't reach

Kazakhstan's geography — vast distances between rural clinics and central hospitals — shaped our design priorities from the start: sensors that are stable without cold-chain storage, readable without specialized technicians, and cheap enough to deploy widely.
